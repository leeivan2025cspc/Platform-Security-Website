Lesson 3: The Human Factor in Cybersecurity

The Role of Humans in Cybersecurity

While technology forms the backbone of cybersecurity, people are often the weakest, and sometimes the strongest-link in any security system.

Why the Human Factor Matters

  • Most security breaches involve human error or manipulation.
  • Even the most advanced systems can fail if users are careless.
  • Cyber attackers often target people via social engineering.

Social Engineering

Social engineering manipulates people into revealing information or granting access through psychological tactics.

Common Forms

  • Phishing: Fake emails tricking users to click malicious links.
  • Spear Phishing: Targeted phishing using personal info.
  • Baiting: Offering “free” items like USBs with malware.
  • Pretexting: Pretending to be IT support to gain access.
  • Tailgating: Following authorized personnel into secure areas.

Insider Threats

Insiders are trusted users who accidentally or intentionally cause harm to an organization’s data or systems.

Types of Insider Threats

  • Malicious Insiders: Intentionally damage or steal data.
  • Negligent Insiders: Careless mistakes expose systems.
  • Compromised Insiders: Credentials stolen by attackers.

Security Awareness and Training

Training and awareness reduce human error and strengthen cybersecurity culture within organizations.

  • Regular workshops and phishing simulations.
  • Clear communication about new threats and policies.
  • Encouraging users to report suspicious activity.
